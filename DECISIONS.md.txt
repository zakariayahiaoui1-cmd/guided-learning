# DECISIONS.md

# Engleasy Guided Learning — Product Decisions

Version: 1.0

---

# Purpose

This document records every important product, UX, marketing and technical decision made during the development of Engleasy Guided Learning.

It exists to answer one question:

> "Why was this built this way?"

Every major decision should be recorded here before implementation.

This prevents future developers (or AI assistants) from accidentally undoing deliberate design decisions.

---

# Decision 001

## The website is NOT a landing page.

Status: ✅ Approved

Date: 2026-06-19

### Decision

The project will be built as a guided learning experience instead of a traditional landing page.

### Reason

Traditional landing pages encourage browsing.

Our users have low digital literacy.

They become overwhelmed when presented with multiple options.

Instead, the website should guide them through one decision at a time.

### Result

The interface behaves like an onboarding flow rather than a marketing page.

---

# Decision 002

## The student is the hero.

Status: ✅ Approved

### Decision

Follow StoryBrand principles.

The student is always the hero.

Engleasy is the guide.

### Reason

People buy transformation, not teachers.

Every section should focus on helping the learner succeed.

Never make Engleasy the center of the story.

---

# Decision 003

## Education before selling.

Status: ✅ Approved

### Decision

Teach first.

Sell later.

### Reason

Trust is the primary conversion mechanism.

The workshop exists to educate.

The course becomes the logical continuation.

Never interrupt education with aggressive selling.

---

# Decision 004

## Three-step guided journey.

Status: ✅ Approved

### Flow

1. Discover your English level.

2. Understand why you're stuck.

3. Continue improving with Engleasy.

### Reason

Three steps are simple enough to remember.

The journey feels achievable.

Progress is visible.

---

# Decision 005

## One visible action at a time.

Status: ✅ Approved

### Decision

Only one accordion should be active.

Only one CTA should be visible.

### Reason

Decision fatigue reduces conversions.

The visitor should never wonder what to do next.

---

# Decision 006

## Arabic-first experience.

Status: ✅ Approved

### Decision

Arabic is the primary experience.

English is fully supported.

### Reason

Most visitors are Algerian Arabic speakers.

The experience should feel native.

English is available without becoming the default.

---

# Decision 007

## Mobile-first design.

Status: ✅ Approved

### Decision

Design for smartphones first.

Desktop is secondary.

### Reason

Most visitors arrive from Instagram and TikTok using mobile devices.

---

# Decision 008

## Google Forms instead of a custom backend.

Status: ✅ Approved (Version 1)

### Decision

Embed Google Forms.

### Reason

Fast deployment.

Reliable.

Zero maintenance.

Automatic data collection.

The architecture should allow replacement with native forms later.

---

# Decision 009

## Fathom replay remains embedded.

Status: ✅ Approved

### Decision

Use embedded Fathom replay.

### Reason

The replay builds authority and trust.

Visitors never leave the website.

---

# Decision 010

## No navigation menu.

Status: ✅ Approved

### Decision

Remove traditional navigation.

### Reason

Every extra navigation option increases the chance that visitors leave the funnel.

The website has one purpose.

Complete the guided journey.

---

# Decision 011

## No workshop duration displayed.

Status: ✅ Approved

### Decision

Hide the workshop duration.

### Reason

A two-hour duration creates unnecessary resistance before users begin watching.

The value matters more than the length.

---

# Decision 012

## Progress creates motivation.

Status: ✅ Approved

### Decision

Display progress visually.

Completed steps receive a green checkmark.

### Reason

Visible progress encourages completion.

People naturally want to finish what they start.

---

# Decision 013

## Emotional reinforcement.

Status: ✅ Approved

### Decision

Celebrate small achievements.

### Examples

"Excellent."

"Great job."

"You're one step closer."

### Reason

Positive reinforcement maintains motivation throughout the journey.

---

# Decision 014

## Course introduction should feel inevitable.

Status: ✅ Approved

### Decision

Never abruptly introduce the course.

Bridge naturally from:

Knowledge

↓

Understanding

↓

Guided practice

↓

Course

### Reason

The paid offer should feel like the logical next step.

# Decision 015

## Premium minimalist design.

Status: ✅ Approved

### Inspiration

Apple

Notion

---

# Decision 016

## No fake urgency.

Status: ✅ Approved

### Decision

Never use false scarcity.

### Allowed

Real enrollment deadlines.

Real seat limits.

Real campaign windows.

### Not Allowed

Fake countdown timers.

Fake "only one seat left" messages.

Misleading urgency.

---

# Decision 017

## Analytics drive future improvements.

Status: ✅ Approved

### Decision

Every important interaction should be measurable.

### Reason

Future decisions should be based on user behavior rather than assumptions.

---

# Decision 018

## Modular architecture.

Status: ✅ Approved

### Decision

Everything should be configurable.

Campaign content.

Branding.

Forms.

Videos.

Analytics.

### Reason

Future campaigns should reuse the same application.

---

# Decision 019

## Human support remains available.

Status: ✅ Approved

### Decision

Include a subtle WhatsApp help option.

### Reason

Some visitors prefer speaking to a real person before registering.

The support option should reassure rather than distract.

---

# Decision 020

## Minimum Lovable Product before optimization.

Status: ✅ Approved

### Decision

Launch Version 0.1 before building advanced features.

### Reason

Real user behavior provides better guidance than speculation.

The first objective is learning, not perfection.

# Guiding Principle

Every decision in this repository should make the website:

* Easier to understand.
* Easier to use.
* Easier to trust.
* More helpful.
* More likely to convert visitors into successful Engleasy students.

When two solutions are equally functional, always choose the one that reduces cognitive effort for the learner.
